Mitochondria are the powerhouses of eukaryotic cells, responsible for producing energy in the form of ATP
.
They have a double membrane structure with an inner and outer membrane, an intermembrane space, and a matrix
.
The inner membrane folds inward to form cristae, increasing surface area for energy production.
Here's a more detailed breakdown of mitochondrial components: Double Membrane:

Outer Membrane

:
Encloses the entire mitochondrion and is relatively permeable
.

Inner Membrane

:
Folded into cristae, which increases surface area for ATP production
.

Intermembrane Space

:
The space between the outer and inner membranes
.
